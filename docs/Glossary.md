# Glossary

Film production terminology used throughout OSFPM.

---

## A

**Above the Line**
Budget section covering the key creative roles — director, writer, producer(s), and principal cast. These costs are negotiated separately and appear above the "line" that divides them from technical/crew costs.

**ADR (Automated Dialogue Replacement)**
Re-recording dialogue in post-production, usually because the location sound was unusable. Actors lip-sync to picture in a studio.

**Art Department**
The team responsible for the visual world of the film — sets, dressing, props, graphics, and anything the camera sees that isn't the actors.

---

## B

**Below the Line**
Budget section covering all crew, equipment, and technical costs below the key creative roles. The vast majority of a production budget.

**Blocking**
Deciding and rehearsing where actors and the camera will move during a scene, before shooting begins.

**Boom Operator**
The sound crew member who holds the boom pole and positions the microphone during shooting. Not to be confused with the Production Sound Mixer who runs the recording equipment.

**Breakdown (Script Breakdown)**
The process of reading the script and cataloguing everything each department needs for each scene — cast, props, costumes, locations, vehicles, special equipment, VFX, etc.

---

## C

**Call Sheet**
The main daily production document. Distributed the evening before each shoot day. Contains call times, location, scenes to be shot, cast requirements, crew contacts, and logistics.

**Call Time**
The time a specific person is required on set or at a location.

**Camera Report**
A per-take log of every clip recorded on a given shoot day. Captures camera setup (model, format, colour space), lens, ISO, shutter speed, scene number, take number, and notes. False takes are marked separately. In OSFPM, the camera report feeds slate and card numbers into the Daily Progress Report.

**Co-producer / Co-production**
An arrangement where two or more separate legal entities share the production budget and associated rights. Each entity's financial share is usually defined as a percentage of the total budget.

**Continuity**
Ensuring that details (costume, props, hair, set dressing) remain consistent between shots that will be cut together in the edit, even if they are filmed days apart.

---

## D

**Daily**
In production, a "daily" is a crew member hired on a day-rate basis rather than a weekly or flat deal.

**Daily Progress Report (DPR)**
An official end-of-day summary documenting what was shot, key timings (crew call, first slate, lunch, wrap), scenes completed and carried forward, talent attendance, media used, and petty cash spend. Also called the "production report" or simply the "daily." In OSFPM, the DPR auto-populates from the call sheet, crew list, camera report, and sound report.

**DIT (Digital Imaging Technician)**
On-set technician responsible for ingesting, backing up, and quality-checking camera files during and after each shoot day.

**Dolly**
A wheeled platform that the camera and camera operator sit on, used to create smooth tracking shots.

---

## F

**False Take**
A take that was aborted before the intended action or performance began — e.g. a camera issue, a stumble, or an aircraft flying over. Logged in the camera report with "FT" in the take column; the next take keeps the same slate number rather than incrementing.

**Flat Deal**
A fixed fee paid for a service or piece of work regardless of time taken, as opposed to a day or week rate.

**Format**
Refers to the type of project (feature film, short, documentary, TV episode, commercial, etc.) and sometimes to the aspect ratio or shooting format (35mm, digital, etc.).

---

## G

**Gaffer**
Head of the lighting department. Works closely with the Director of Photography to light each scene.

**Genre**
The category or type of story (drama, comedy, thriller, horror, documentary, etc.).

**Grip**
A crew member responsible for camera support equipment — dollies, cranes, jibs, track. The Key Grip leads the grip department.

---

## I

**Invoice**
A bill from a supplier or freelancer. In OSFPM, invoices are logged in the invoice tracker and can be linked to a budget line to track actual spend vs. planned spend.

---

## L

**Location**
Any real-world place used for filming, as opposed to a purpose-built set. The Locations department scouts, secures, and manages all filming locations.

**Line Producer**
The producer responsible for the day-to-day running of the production and for managing the budget. Acts as the financial and logistical bridge between the creative team and the crew.

---

## M

**Martini Shot**
The last shot of the shoot day (so called because the next drink is a martini). Also called the "window shot" — you can see the window out of which you'll be throwing your gear.

**MixPre**
A range of portable field audio recorders made by Sound Devices, widely used for production sound recording. At the end of each shoot day the MixPre generates a CSV report listing every recorded file with metadata (scene, take, circled, file name, duration, track names). OSFPM imports this CSV in the Sound Report.

---

## P

**Pages**
Script pages are used as a measure of how much content is being shot. A standard screenplay page equals roughly one minute of screen time.

**Pre-Production**
The planning phase before shooting begins. Includes budgeting, scheduling, casting, scouting locations, building sets, and department prep.

**Post-Production**
Everything that happens after shooting wraps — editing, VFX, colour grading, sound mix, music, and delivery.

**Production**
The shooting period. Also used to refer to the company or team making the film.

**Production Report**
An official daily record of what was shot, how much, and any significant events. Generated by the production office at the end of each shoot day.

**Props**
Objects handled by actors or placed in the scene by the Art Department that are not fixed set dressing.

---

## R

**Rate**
The fee charged per unit of work — per day, per week, per hour, or as a flat deal.

---

## S

**Script Supervisor**
Also called "continuity." Responsible for maintaining script continuity across all takes and shots, and for keeping a detailed log of what was shot each day.

**Shooting Script**
The final, locked version of a screenplay that is distributed to the full cast and crew for production. Subsequent revisions are distributed on coloured paper following the WGA/DGA sequence: white (initial lock), blue, pink, yellow, green, goldenrod, buff, salmon, cherry, tan, ivory — then repeating. OSFPM tracks shooting script revisions and assigns these colours automatically.

**Sides**
A cut-down version of the script for the day — only the scenes being filmed that day. Printed small for actors and director to carry on set.

**Stinger**
An extension lead / power cable used on set. Not a bee.

---

## T

**Treatment**
A document describing the story and tone of a film, usually a few pages, written before the full screenplay.

---

## U

**Unit**
In budgeting, the unit defines what "one of something" means for a rate: a day, a week, a flat fee, an hour, a roll, etc.

---

## W

**Wrap**
The end of a shoot — either the end of the day ("that's a wrap for today") or the end of the entire production ("that's a picture wrap").

---

*More terms will be added as OSFPM's features expand.*
